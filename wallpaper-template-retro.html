QUANTUM PHYSICS

WAVE-PARTICLE DUALITY

Light exhibits both wave and particle properties depending on how it is observed.

  • Photons behave as both waves and particles
  • Double-slit experiment demonstrates interference
  • Observation affects quantum states

⚡ The act of measurement fundamentally changes the system being observed.

E = hf

where:
h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s
(Planck's constant)
► KEY INSIGHT

Einstein's photoelectric effect proved light consists of discrete packets of energy called photons.
